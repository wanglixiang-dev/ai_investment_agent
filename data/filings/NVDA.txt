NVIDIA designs graphics processing units, accelerated computing platforms, and AI infrastructure products for data center, gaming, professional visualization, automotive, and OEM markets.

Revenue growth depends heavily on data center demand, AI accelerator adoption, cloud service provider capital expenditure, and enterprise AI workloads.

Key risks include supply constraints, export controls, dependence on semiconductor manufacturing partners, competition from custom AI chips, and cyclicality in gaming and data center demand.

Gross margin can be affected by product mix, memory costs, supply chain constraints, and pricing pressure from competitors.

Liquidity is supported by operating cash flow, cash reserves, and strong demand for accelerated computing products.
